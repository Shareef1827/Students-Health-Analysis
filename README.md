# Students-Health-Analysis
This project focuses on analyzing student health and mental well-being data to understand how factors such as academic pressure, sleep duration, dietary habits, financial stress, and lifestyle patterns influence depression levels among students.

## Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing student health and mental well-being data to understand how factors such as academic pressure, sleep duration, dietary habits, financial stress, and lifestyle patterns influence depression levels among students.

The analysis combines Python-based EDA, SQL-driven business queries, and Power BI dashboarding to deliver actionable insights.

## Problem Statement
Mental health issues among students are increasing globally.
This project aims to:
- Identify patterns related to student depression
- Understand the impact of sleep, stress, diet, and academic pressure
- Support data-driven decision-making for educators and institutions

## Dataset Information
- Total Records: 27,900+ students
### Key Features:
- Demographics: Age, Gender, City, Degree, Profession
- Lifestyle: Sleep Duration, Dietary Habits, Work/Study Hours
- Stress Indicators: Academic Pressure, Work Pressure, Financial Stress
- Mental Health: Depression, Suicidal Thoughts, Family Mental Health History

## Tools & Technologies
- Python: Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn
- SQL: MySQL (Joins, CASE statements, Aggregations, Subqueries)
- Power BI: Data modeling, DAX, Interactive dashboards
- Jupyter Notebook: EDA & data preprocessing

## Project Workflow
1) Data Understanding & Cleaning
2) Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
3) SQL Business Analysis
4) Dashboard Design & Visualization
5) Insight Generation & Interpretation

### Bivariate & Multivariate Analysis
- Less than 5 hours of sleep is associated with higher depression levels
- Academic and work pressure show a positive correlation with depression
- Unhealthy dietary habits are linked to higher depression rates
- Certain degrees and professions experience higher academic stress
- City-wise and gender-wise differences exist in student health patterns
